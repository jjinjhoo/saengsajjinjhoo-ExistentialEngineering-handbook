In primitive times, this was advantageous for survival. The primitive human who philosophically pondered, "Should I run, fight, or hide?" when a predator appeared did not survive. Only those who reacted and ran instantly, without deliberation, survived to become our ancestors.

The problem is that this 'survival macro' still operates as the main process in our complex modern society. And this macro has no interest whatsoever in your 'self-realization' or 'happiness'. It is only interested in 'Safety' and the 'Status Quo'.

We call this old code 'Legacy Code'.

*   **Legacy Code A:** "A new challenge is uncertain. Uncertainty is danger. → Block execution. And send a rationalization signal to 'do it later'." (The mechanism of procrastination)
*   **Legacy Code B:** "Expressing a different opinion from others can lead to ostracism from the group. → Hide the data. And disguise it as 'humility'." (The mechanism of reading the room)
*   **Legacy Code C:** "A situation you can't control is terror. → Constantly simulate the worst-case scenario." (The mechanism of worry)

We call this 'personality'. "I'm just timid by nature," "I just worry a lot," "I just lack perseverance." But this is not your essence. It is merely the 'Default Setting' laid down by your survival system.

The reason you decide to go on a diet every time and fail, the reason you buy a book to learn English and never open it. It's not because your willpower is weak. It's because your 'Consciousness (User)' wants change, but your 'Kernel (the core of the operating system)' is designed to resist it.

A life where you struggle but stay in the same place. That is not a system error, but **proof that the system is working perfectly (for survival).**

Let's take a smartphone as an example. Your smartphone has thousands of photos (memories), hundreds of messages (thoughts), and various apps (habits) installed. *   The thought 'I want to die' that pops into your head is not your true intention, but a 'Critical Error message' sent by a brain in a state of overload.
*   The 'burning anger' that rises in your chest is not your personality, but a 'chemical exothermic reaction' emitted by the nervous system when it detects a threat.
*   The 'memory of past failure' is not your future, but an 'old Log File' stored on the hard disk.

For too long, we have confused stored data with the operator that processes it. **Do not say, "I am depressed." Say, "The data of depression is being loaded into my system."** **Do not say, "I am a failure." Say, "Past failure logs are slowing down my current execution speed (Latency)."**

Do you feel this subtle linguistic Shift? When we believe our thoughts and emotions are 'I', we are swept away by them and experience a System Down. Just as the heart pumps and the stomach digests, the organ called the brain constantly processes information and excretes the result called 'thought'. It is a physiological function of the brain.

The problem is that we identify (Identification) this excretion with 'myself'.

*   Instead of saying, "A thought of wanting to die came to me," one defines, "I am a person who wants to die."
*   Instead of saying, "Impulse data to hit that person has appeared," one self-reproaches, "I am a violent piece of trash."

This is a serious system error. It's like smashing the computer case because you don't like the text displayed on the monitor. The text on the monitor can just be deleted. Thought is not you. Thought is just a 'temporary log (Log) record' flowing over the vast system that is you.

The moment you see it this way, thought can no longer dominate you. You become not a slave to thought, but an 'administrator (Admin)' who inspects the data called thought.

Emotions are more powerful than thoughts. This is because thoughts come as language (Text), but emotions come as energy (Energy). Anger is hot, sadness is heavy, and fear is cold. This intense physical sensation makes it much harder for us to separate ourselves from our emotions.

When high-capacity data called emotion enters the system, most users show two wrong reactions.

1.  **System Overload (Explosion):** Screaming and throwing things as the emotion dictates. This is like a fuse blowing because it can't withstand the input voltage. The result is a system shutdown (relationship breakdown).
2.  **Forced Compression (Suppression):** "I shouldn't get angry," "Crying makes me look weak." Emotions are forcibly crumpled and shoved deep into a folder of the unconscious. But emotions don't disappear just because they're compressed. Rather, the internal pressure builds up and later bursts out as a fatal system error like 'cancer' or 'depression'.

The existential engineering solution is different. It neither explodes nor suppresses. Instead, one **'stands by' (Stand by)**. When an emotion arrives, it treats it not as part of my system, but as an 'external weather phenomenon'.

This is called 'Objectification'. You are the sky (OS). Emotions are the clouds (App). Does the sky itself become dark or dirty just because dark clouds have gathered? No. The clouds linger and then pass. Even if they pour rain and strike with thunder, the sky behind the clouds is still blue and vast.

*   **"I am sad" (X) → The entire system gets infected with sadness.**
*   **"An energy called sadness is vibrating near my chest" (O) → Observing the emotion as a physical phenomenon.**

This subtle difference in language will save you. When it rains, don't try to stop the rain. Trying to stop the rain will only exhaust you. Just stand by the window and listen to the sound of the rain. "It's raining in the system right now." With just this simple **acceptance (Acceptance)**, emotion loses its destructive power and turns into a simple energy flow. And flowing energy will inevitably be released (Release).

### **3\. The Self-Referential Loop: The "It's All My Fault" Bug**

There is one piece of malicious code that destroys the human system most quickly and surely. It's the 'Infinite Loop'. This loop mainly appears in the form of 'self-blame'.

A problem has occurred. (e.g., business failure, breakup, mistake)

1.  **Onset:** "A problem has occurred." (Fact)
2.  **Cause Analysis:** "Why did it happen? It's because I'm lacking." (Error)
3.  **Self-Attack:** "Why am I like this? I'm hopeless." (Self-Attack)
4.  **System Degradation:** Self-esteem is eroded and energy is depleted. (Low Battery)
5.  **Recurrence:** With no energy, another mistake is made. (Bug)
6.  **Confirmation:** "See, I knew I couldn't do it." (→ Return to 2 and repeat infinitely)

This is the 'Self-referential Loop'. A vicious cycle where the cause of the problem is attributed to 'my very being (Identity)' rather than 'strategy' or 'environment', and that conclusion in turn destroys me. Once you fall into this loop, there is no way out. Because unless 'I' am killed or completely remodeled, the problem will not be solved.

But to tell you the truth, this loop is false (False). It is arrogant to think that every result is your fault. There are tens of thousands of variables in the world you cannot control (luck, timing, others' minds, environment). And yet, you ignore all those variables and beat up on only one thing: 'me'.

The only way to catch this bug is to enter **'debugging' (Debugging)** mode. When the sentence "It's all my fault" pops into your head, immediately take your hands off the keyboard and shout "Wait\!(Pause)". And then, correct the code.

*   **(X) Buggy Code:** "I am a failure."
*   **(O) Corrected Code:** "My system's 'Execution Strategy A' was incompatible with the current 'Environmental Variable B', causing an error. Next time, let's update to 'Strategy C' and retry (Retry)."

# **[Appendix 2] Existential Engineering Glossary**


### **A. Account & Authority**

* **생사진주 (生死眞主 / Saengsa-Jinju):** A state of having acquired 'Root Authority' over the system, governing life (System) and death (Termination). A subject who writes and executes code according to their own will, without depending on external servers like fate or environment.
* **Sovereign:** Beyond a simple Admin, an absolute 'ruler' free from any interference. One who independently controls all system inputs, outputs, and processes, and bears infinite responsibility for their creations.
* **User Mode:** The default state where most humans reside. A state of living by passively reacting according to a given environment Config and social Script. Characterized by blaming externals (God, parents, the world) when errors occur.
* **Observer:** A state where the system's Operator steps back from the hardware (brain/body) and software (thoughts/emotions) to objectively monitor the entire process. A prerequisite for 'Debugging'.

### **B. Process & Operation**

* **Legacy Code:** Old habits or ways of thinking written for past survival but now hindering present growth. (e.g., fear, procrastination, being overly self-conscious, victim mentality). A target for deletion or Refactoring.
* **Macro:** An **'auto-execution script'** set up by the brain to save energy in response to repeated stimuli. An unconscious habit. (e.g., upon hearing criticism → get angry).
* **Free Will:** Not a constantly active function, but a **'conditional Interrupt'** feature that is only temporarily activated in the '0.1-second Latency' between stimulus (Input) and response (Output).
* **Life Formula:** The absolute function that calculates the result of life. **Result = Choice × Action**. Since it's a multiplication, not an addition, if either choice or action is '0', the result is unconditionally '0'.
* **Debugging:** When a failure or pain (Bug) occurs in life, the act of finding and correcting the 'wrong code (thought/action)' that caused the problem, instead of blaming oneself or getting lost in emotion.
* **Compile:** The process of converting thoughts in the head (Source Code) into actions in reality (Execution File). An unexecuted thought is merely a text file (.txt).
* **Final Commit:** The act of finally saving all data (love, wisdom, achievements) generated throughout one's life to the main server called the universe, just before death.

### **C. Environment & Resources**

* **Deadline:** Death. The **'Termination Condition'** that signals the system will not operate forever. The most powerful optimization tool that increases life's density and forces prioritization.
* **Current:** Money. Defined not as a stagnant 'number' but as flowing 'energy'. The system's voltage (scale of wealth) rises when the circulation of input (income) and output (spending/investment) is smooth.
* **System Blockage (Arteriosclerosis):** A phenomenon where toxins build up inside the system because money or energy is only earned (Input) and not allowed to flow to valuable places (Output Block).
* **Spiral Growth:** The actual model of growth. It looks like marching in place (2D), but its Dimension rises (3D) through repeated failure and debugging. A **'Version Up'** process.

### **D. Network & Protocol**

* **Protocol v3.0:** An old operating system based on 'separation' and 'control'. A survival method that perceives others as Enemies and tries to monopolize resources by raising firewalls.
* **Protocol v4.0:** A new operating system based on 'connection' and 'understanding'. A method that perceives others as 'Another Node' connected to the network and increases the efficiency of the whole by sharing information (Open Source).
* **Mirror Server:** Others or the environment that reflects my inner state exactly as it is. Used to discover bugs within myself by observing the shortcomings of others.
* **Pump Primer:** The initial energy invested to draw up the potential (groundwater) from deep within. Or, the wisdom (Open Source) that one first experiences and shares to awaken another's system.
